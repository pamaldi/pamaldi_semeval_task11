## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Figure Errors** (MOST COMMON!):
   - Don't assume the figure - calculate it from M's positions
   - Figure 1: M=subject in P1, M=predicate in P2
   - Figure 2: M=predicate in P1, M=predicate in P2
   - Figure 3: M=subject in P1, M=subject in P2
   - Figure 4: M=predicate in P1, M=subject in P2

2. **Middle Term Errors**:
   - M MUST be in both premises
   - M must NOT be in conclusion
   - If M is in conclusion, you identified it wrong

3. **Term Identification**:
   - ALWAYS derive S and P from conclusion FIRST
   - Major term (P) = predicate of conclusion
   - Minor term (S) = subject of conclusion

4. **Proposition Type Errors**:
   - A = "All S are P" (universal affirmative)
   - E = "No S is P" (universal negative)
   - I = "Some S are P" (particular affirmative)
   - O = "Some S are not P" (particular negative)
   - Watch for: "Not all" = O, "A portion of" = I, "There are no" = E

5. **Premise/Conclusion Confusion**:
   - Conclusion is LAST (after "therefore", "thus", etc.)
   - Don't swap premises with conclusion

## CRITICAL: Distinguishing Proposition Types

| Keyword | Type | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| "Every X is Y" | **A** | "Every bird is an animal" |
| "All X are Y" | **A** | "All birds are animals" |
| "No X is Y" | **E** | "No bird is a fish" |
| "Some X are Y" | **I** | "Some birds are pets" |
| "Some X are not Y" | **O** | "Some birds are not pets" |
| "Not all X are Y" | **O** | "Not all birds fly" |

**WARNING:** 
- "Every X is Y" is ALWAYS type A (affirmative), never E
- "Some X are not Y" is ALWAYS type O (negative), not I
- The negation is in the COPULA ("are not"), not in the predicate
